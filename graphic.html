Graphic

Graphic cues are the use of size/scale, style, and color to portray hierarchy. Of course, many artists will make use of graphic and special cues in order to display hierarchy, but there are some artists that exemplify graphic cues when creating hierarchy. Michael Bierut is a current artist born in 1957. He uses scale/size and color in his design work. In some of his designs, his body text is extremely small compared to the header text. By making the body text extremely small in comparison to the header text, Michael can portray hierarchy through scale. Herbert Bayer was an Austrian graphic designer that studied at the Bauhaus and contributed to many Bauhaus publications. He displays hierarchy using color, style, and scale.
